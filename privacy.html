Privacy Policy

Effective July 19, 2026 · Applies to Marquee for macOS

Marquee collects nothing about you. Almost everything it does happens on your Mac.

Data we collect

None. Marquee does not collect, transmit, store, or sell any personal data. There are no analytics, no telemetry, no crash-reporting services, no advertising identifiers, no accounts, and no tracking of any kind. The app ships an Apple privacy manifest declaring exactly this.

Your content

Screenshots, project files, headlines, captions, translations, and any fonts or images you add are processed entirely on your Mac. Marquee reads only the files you explicitly choose and writes only where you tell it to.

On-device Vision analysis (reading text and color from your screenshots) and Apple Intelligence copy drafting, when available, run locally on your device. Your content is never sent to us or to any third party for these features.

The ASO audit

Marquee's App Store Optimization audit is deterministic and runs entirely on your Mac against the search terms you type in. It uses no live App Store data and sends nothing off your device.

App Store Connect uploads

Publishing is optional. If you connect an App Store Connect API key, that key is stored only in your Mac's Keychain and never leaves your machine. When you choose to upload, Marquee talks directly to Apple's App Store Connect API over an encrypted connection, authenticated with short-lived tokens signed locally on your Mac. There is no Marquee server in between — your files go to your own Apple account and nowhere else.

What we could see

Nothing. Marquee operates no servers and receives no data from the app. We could not access your screenshots, keys, or uploads even if we wanted to.

Children

Marquee is a professional developer tool and is not directed at children. It collects no data from anyone.

Changes

If this policy ever changes, the new version will be posted at this address with a new effective date. Because the app is built around on-device processing, we do not expect it to change in substance.
